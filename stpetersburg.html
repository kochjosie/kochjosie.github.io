The St. Petersburg Paradox

The St. Petersburg Paradox is a famous problem in decision making analysis. Prior to this paradox, the rational decision was defined as one that maximized expected value. However, when presented with this game, players are never willing to pay the expected value to play. So, are all humans irrational or did economists need to update their definition?
